EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS - KEY FINDINGS
============================================================

1. Disease prevalence: 49.5% of patients have cardiovascular disease (cardio=1), 50.5% do not. Dataset is well-balanced for modeling.

2. Disease risk rises sharply with age: from 23.4% in the 30-40 group to 67.0% in the 61-65 group.

3. Patients with disease show notably higher blood pressure: average systolic 134 mmHg vs 120 mmHg in healthy patients; diastolic 85 vs 78 mmHg.

4. Cholesterol is strongly linked to risk: 43.5% disease rate at normal levels vs 76.2% at well-above-normal levels.

5. Glucose shows a similar but weaker pattern: 47.6% at normal levels vs 61.8% at well-above-normal levels.

6. Lifestyle factors show smaller individual effects: smokers have a 46.8% disease rate vs 49.7% for non-smokers (a modest, somewhat counterintuitive gap, likely confounded by age and other factors in self-reported data).

7. Physically active patients have a 48.5% disease rate vs 53.3% for inactive patients, a small protective effect.

8. Top correlates with disease risk: ap_hi (0.43), ap_lo (0.34), age_years (0.24), cholesterol (0.22), weight (0.18).
