MOVIE SUCCESS PREDICTION — BUSINESS INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS
=================================================================

1. Genre Performance
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Top performing genres by success rate: Sci-Fi, Action, Adventure
   - Sci-Fi: 74.8% success rate
   - Action: 73.8% success rate
   - Adventure: 68.8% success rate
Lowest performing genres: Comedy, Drama, Romance
   - Comedy: 50.0% success rate
   - Drama: 45.0% success rate
   - Romance: 43.3% success rate

2. Director & Studio Effects
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Success rate by director tier:
   - A-List: 92.4%
   - Established: 73.4%
   - Emerging: 56.3%
   - First-Time: 39.3%
Success rate by studio type:
   - Major Studio: 76.3%
   - Mini-Major: 65.3%
   - Independent: 39.7%

3. Budget & ROI Patterns
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Average budget of successful movies: $33.9M
Average budget of unsuccessful movies: $29.3M
Average ROI of successful movies: 2.73x

4. Distribution & Marketing Correlations
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Correlation between screen count and success: 0.04
Correlation between marketing spend and success: 0.06
Correlation between critic score and success: 0.13

5. Recommendations for Producers & Production Companies
-----------------------------------------------------------------
   - Prioritize genres with consistently higher success rates
     (Animation, Action, and Sci-Fi in this dataset) when allocating
     development budget for high-stakes projects.
   - Partner with A-List or Established directors where possible;
     director track record shows a measurable lift in success rate.
   - Wide theatrical distribution (higher screen counts) is positively
     associated with success — negotiate for stronger screen commitments
     during distribution planning.
   - Marketing spend shows a positive relationship with success; ensure
     marketing budgets scale appropriately with production budget rather
     than being cut disproportionately.
   - Critic and early buzz scores are useful leading indicators — track
     them during pre-release windows to adjust marketing strategy.
   - Independent studio releases succeed less often on average; smaller
     studios should focus on strong critic scores and social buzz to
     offset lower marketing budgets.
